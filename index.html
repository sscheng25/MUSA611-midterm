Introduction to National Parks

National parks are defined as a reserve of natural, semi-natural, or developed land that a sovereign state declares or owns, with the conservation of ‘wild nature’ for posterity and as a symbol of national pride.

Since 1872 that the US government established the first “public park or pleasuring-ground for the benefit and enjoyment of the people”, Yellowstone National Park, there has been 423 recreational sites in the National Park System (NPS), while about 60 of those are what we often called national parks. During the past 150 years, over 15 billion visitors are fascinated by the natural beauty of those parks and we really treasure those gifts from our mother nature.

National Park Service - NPS

There are 427 parks in the National Park Service system, types of which includes national parks, national historical park, national monuments, national seashore, etc.

National Parks - History

In 1872, Yellowstone became the first national park, signed by President Ulysses S. Grant. It was also the world's first national park. Thus, we can say that there is a history of 150 years for US national parks.

In 1906, President Theodore Roosevelt signed the Antiquities Act, which gave presidents the authority to create national monuments to preserve areas of natural or historic interest on public lands.

The National Park Service was officially established in 1916.

National Parks - Visitors 2021

In 2021, 297,115,406 recreational visits are made throughout the NPS parks. Three parks had more than 10 million recreation visits, 73 parks had more than one million recreation visits (19% of reporting parks), which is up from 60 parks in 2020 and down from 80 parks in 2019.

Independence National Historical Park

Philly is famous as the birth place of the United States, where the founding fathers of the country met here to sign the Declaration of Independence in 1776 at the Second Continental Congress, and the Constitution at the Philadelphia Convention of 1787. Both documents were debated and signed inside Independence Hall, now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a US National Historical Park.

Great Smoky Mountains National Park

The park straddles the ridgeline of the Great Smoky Mountains, part of the Blue Ridge Mountains, which are a division of the larger Appalachian Mountain chain. It was chartered by the United States Congress in 1934, and officially dedicated by President Roosevelt in 1940.

The Great Smoky Mountains is the most visited national parks in 2021, attracted over 14.1 million visitors.

Zion National Park

Zion National Park locates in southwestern Utah near the town of Springdale. The highlight of Zion is an expansive canyon and the canyon walls are reddish and tan-colored Navajo Sandstone eroded by the North Fork of the Virgin River.

Zion National Park is the second most visited national parks in 2021, attracted over 5 million visitors.

Yellow Stone National Park

Yellowstone National Park is a nearly 3,500-sq.-mile wilderness recreation area atop a volcanic hot spot. Mostly in Wyoming, the park spreads into parts of Montana and Idaho too. Yellowstone features dramatic canyons, alpine rivers, lush forests, hot springs and gushing geysers, including its most famous, Old Faithful. It's also home to hundreds of animal species, including bears, wolves, bison, elk and antelope.

Yellow Stone National Park is the third most visited national parks in 2021, attracted over 4.9 million visitors. It is also the first national park of the US and famous worldwide.

Grand Canyon National Park

Grand Canyon National Park, in Arizona, is home to much of the immense Grand Canyon, with its layered bands of red rock revealing millions of years of geological history. Viewpoints include Mather Point, Yavapai Observation Station and architect Mary Colter’s Lookout Studio and her Desert View Watchtower. Lipan Point, with wide views of the canyon and Colorado River, is a popular, especially at sunrise and sunset.

Grand Canyon National Park is the forth most visited national parks in 2021, attracted about 4.5 million visitors.
